The gut détente

A negotiated standoff maintained by three interlocking agreements. The default is war.

1
The barrier compact
Epithelial cells maintain physical separation. Paneth cells sterilize the crypt. Commensals stay luminal. In return, commensals get habitat and protection. The DMZ reduces information flow — the truce depends partly on managed ignorance.
Enforced by: defensins, mucus layer, IgA, tight junctions
Weakened by: NOD2 mutations, defensin deficiency, dysbiosis, barrier-damaging drugs
2
The tolerance pact
Tregs actively suppress effector responses to commensal antigens. DCs are conditioned to be tolerogenic. Commensals subsidize their own policing via butyrate → Treg differentiation. When the barrier weakens and antigen load rises, DCs flip from tolerogenic (TGF-β → Tregs) to inflammatory (IL-23 → Th17). The pact doesn't just fail — it inverts.
Enforced by: Tregs, IL-10, TGF-β, tolerogenic DCs, commensal butyrate
Inverted by: barrier breach → DC reprogramming → IL-23 production → Th17 differentiation
3
The structural ceasefire
Fibroblasts remain quiescent. Repair activates only transiently after acute injury. When chronic inflammation produces sustained TL1A/TGF-β, fibroblasts activate into myofibroblasts and acquire autocrine survival loops. They become a new autonomous player who refuses to disband — a militia with institutional momentum.
Enforced by: absence of chronic activating signals
Broken by: chronic TL1A/TGF-β → fibroblast autonomy → irreversible fibrosis
The coordination trap
Barrier weakens → more antigen pressure on tolerance → tolerance inverts (Tregs replaced by Th17) → chronic inflammation → fibroblast activation → structural remodeling → further barrier damage → more antigen → ... Each player is behaving rationally given what every other player is doing. No one can unilaterally return to the healthy equilibrium. The disease state is self-maintaining. Escaping the trap requires changing the payoff matrix for multiple players simultaneously — which is the mechanistic argument for combination therapy and early intervention before fibroblasts become autonomous.
