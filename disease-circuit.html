Series circuit, parallel circuit, corrosive element

A circuit-based framework for understanding why some immune diseases respond to monotherapy

Psoriasis Near-series circuit Structurally passive tissue

Predominantly serial: IL-23 → Th17 → IL-17A/F → keratinocytes → plaque. Blocking IL-23 captures most of the signal. However, a minor IL-23-independent bypass exists — γδ T cells and ILC3s can produce IL-17F via IL-1β/IL-6, which is why bimekizumab (dual IL-17A/F) achieves higher PASI 100 rates than IL-23 blockade alone. No fibrosis or structural remodeling, but CD8+ TRM cells persist in resolved plaques and drive site-specific recurrence. Sustained IL-23 blockade may gradually deplete TRM (they depend on IL-23 for survival) — the basis for treatment-free remission data.

Near-series with a minor bypass. Structurally passive (no fibrosis) but immunologically memoried (TRM). Sustained IL-23 blockade may erase TRM → treatment-free remission potential.

Atopic dermatitis Partially parallel Mildly active tissue

Dominated by type 2 (T2) inflammation: TSLP → Th2 → IL-4/IL-13, with IgE/mast cell and Th22/IL-22 branches running in partial parallel. IL-4Rα blockade (dupilumab) captures most of the T2 signal. Barrier defect (filaggrin) is constitutive in many patients and creates a mild feed-forward, but the skin remains structurally reversible — no fibrosis, no remodeling.

T2-dominant with weaker parallel branches. Barrier defect is constitutive but tissue remains structurally reversible. IL-4Rα blockade captures the bulk of T2 signal.

IBD (ileal Crohn's) Parallel + cross-talk + corrosion Actively destructive tissue

Multiple immune pathways in true parallel with active compensatory cross-talk — block one and others upregulate. The tissue is a co-conspirator: constitutive innate barrier defects (including Paneth cell α-defensin deficiency) allow microbial translocation that initiates and perpetuates inflammation. The microbiome is a disease driver. Chronic inflammation triggers fibrosis and structural remodeling that is no longer immunologically reversible. Two windows close: immunological (effector memory) and structural (fibrosis/stricture). The structural window closes faster and more permanently.

True parallel with compensatory cross-talk, constitutive barrier defect, and a corrosive element (fibrosis) that makes damage irreversible. Monotherapy blocks one wire; others compensate and corrosion continues. Requires combination + early aggressive intervention.

Rheumatoid arthritis Parallel circuit Moderately active tissue

Fully parallel immune pathways (TNF, IL-6, T cell costimulation, B cells) — four distinct mechanisms validated by approved drugs. The synovium is a moderate co-participant: fibroblast-like synoviocytes (FLS) can become autonomously invasive as "pannus," driving joint erosion independent of the initiating immune trigger. Less compartmentalized than the gut but the pannus creates a structural window analogous to intestinal fibrosis. JAK inhibition sits at a common junction box across pathways.

Fully parallel (4 validated mechanisms). Moderately active tissue — pannus creates a structural window like fibrosis in Crohn's but tissue is less compartmentalized. JAK inhibition = junction box across paths.

Framework summary

PsO = near-series, structurally passive but immunologically memoried (TRM) → monotherapy sufficient; sustained blockade may erase tissue memory (near-cure).

AD = partially parallel (T2-dominant), mildly active tissue (filaggrin) → dominant-path blockade usually enough; barrier creates mild feed-forward but no structural irreversibility.

RA = fully parallel, moderately active tissue (pannus) → multiple validated mechanisms; structural window creates urgency for treat-to-target. JAK as junction-box alternative.

IBD = fully parallel with cross-talk, constitutive barrier defect + microbiome co-driver + irreversible fibrosis → combination therapy AND early aggressive intervention required to outrun both immunological and structural windows. The most complex circuit.
