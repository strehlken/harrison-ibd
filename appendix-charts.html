APPENDIX C  ·  CLINICAL DATA

Reading the Smoke

Harrison’s laboratory and imaging data, visualized. Each chart is a different instrument trained on the canyon — measuring the siege from outside its walls.

Fecal calprotectin
The dead-conscript count · μg/g stool · Normal < 50

The smoke above the canyon. Calprotectin measures dead neutrophils washing out of the intestine. A level of 4,300 in March 2025 triggered the colonoscopy that found pancolonic inflammation. Eight months after starting risankizumab: 25. The conscripts have stopped coming.

White blood cell count
The body’s overall mobilization · ×1000/μL · Normal 4.6–10.4

The Marrow’s production rate. WBC drifted above the normal ceiling through 2023–2024 — the body mobilizing more immune cells in response to sustained demand from the canyon. By April 2025, after treatment initiation, the count settled into the normal range. The Marrow is standing down.

Eosinophils
Ruling out what this isn’t · % of WBC · Normal 0.9–6.8%

The line that didn’t move. Eosinophils stayed at the bottom of the normal range throughout — confirming this was never a type 2 (allergic) war. No eosinophilic infiltration, no parasitic component. This is Th1/Th17 inflammation: soldiers and conscripts, not the eosinophil corps. Consistent with Crohn’s.

Bowel wall thickness by segment
Watching the Builders · cm · June 2, 2025 ultrasound at Mt. Sinai · Abnormal ≥ 0.30 cm

The scope saw UC-like surface inflammation across the entire colon. The ultrasound saw the walls — and found a right-sided gradient. The left colon (descending, sigmoid) has paper-thin walls. The right colon and terminal ileum are two to three times thicker. This is a Crohn’s pattern: right-sided predominance with ileal involvement. The terminal ileum — the segment the scope called “normal” — has the thickest wall of any segment measured. The scope sees the paint. The ultrasound sees the wall.
