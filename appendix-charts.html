Reading the Smoke

Harrison’s laboratory and imaging data, visualized. Each chart is a different instrument trained on the canyon — measuring the siege from outside its walls.

Fecal calprotectin
The dead-conscript count · μg/g stool · Normal < 50 · Full history since diagnosis

When the Wall-Folk are in distress, they scream — a chemical called IL-8 that summons neutrophils from the bloodstream. These conscripts live for hours. They arrive, dump their calprotectin cargo into the tissue, and die. The calprotectin washes downstream and out of the body. Measuring it in stool is like counting spent shell casings washed out of the canyon. It is the smoke above the city.

The full arc. Two sieges: May 2022 (4,080) and March 2025 (4,300). Between them, a long smolder on Pentasa that never fully quieted — the canyon never went below 666. After risankizumab: 102 at three months (borderline — the fighting is subsiding), then 25 at eight months (normal — the conscripts have stopped coming). The pattern reveals that the March 2025 spike was not a sudden event. It was a recurrence. The canyon had been here before.

The second siege: what the full history reveals

With the complete calprotectin record, the March 2025 spike looks different than it did when we could only see two years of data. It was not a sudden escalation from a smolder. It was a recurrence. May 2022 had hit 4,080 — nearly identical. That first siege was followed by a colonoscopy that found no ulcers, and Pentasa was increased from two to three capsules per day. The calprotectin dropped to 666 by November, then drifted upward through 2023 (1,260 → 1,100) and gradually down to 789 by August 2024 — a long smolder, never quiet, never in crisis. Then the second spike.

The pattern tells you something the individual numbers don’t: this canyon’s floor is not zero. It is not 50. The lowest calprotectin ever recorded on Pentasa was 666 — still thirteen times the normal threshold. The treatment was dampening the fire, not extinguishing it. The system was always capable of re-igniting because the underlying inflammatory drive was never fully controlled. The second siege was not a surprise. It was the inevitable consequence of a smoldering war managed with a therapy that could not achieve deep suppression.

As for external triggers — an intercurrent infection, antibiotics, NSAIDs, or simply disease progression outpacing the drug — any of these could have tipped the balance. But the deeper answer is structural: Pentasa was never the right tool for this canyon. Risankizumab achieved what Pentasa could not: a calprotectin of 25. Below normal. For the first time in Harrison’s recorded history.

Absolute neutrophil count
The conscript reserve · ×1000/μL · Normal 1.8–5.6 (Yale) / 1.8–7.8 (Sinai)

If calprotectin measures dead conscripts in the waste stream, ANC measures live conscripts in the blood — the standing army at the staging area, mobilized by the Marrow and waiting for deployment. During a severe flare, the canyon consumes neutrophils faster than the Marrow can replace them. The blood count can actually drop even as the tissue is flooded with dying conscripts. It is like measuring soldiers at the staging area versus spent shell casings on the battlefield: during a massive battle, the staging area empties out because the battle is consuming them.

The reserve shrinks steadily from above-normal to solidly mid-range. By November 2025, the Marrow is producing conscripts at peacetime rates. The staging area is quiet because the canyon is no longer consuming them.

White blood cell count
The body’s overall mobilization · ×1000/μL · Normal 4.5–11.4

The broadest measure of the Marrow’s output. WBC counts every immune cell type in the blood — conscripts, soldiers, Taster precursors, Peacekeepers, all of them. When the canyon is at war, the Marrow runs hot and the count rises. When the fighting subsides, production cools.

Above normal for two years. Then a steady descent under treatment, with the June dip to 5 reflecting early risankizumab effect. By November, settled at 7 — solidly mid-range. The Marrow has stood down.

Monocytes (absolute count)
Taster and macrophage precursor production · ×1000/μL · Normal 0.38–0.88 (Yale) / 0.3–0.9 (Sinai)

Monocytes are not conscripts. They are the raw material that becomes Tasters and macrophages — the intelligence officers and the heavy-duty cleanup crew. When the canyon demands more surveillance and more reporting, the Marrow dispatches more monocyte precursors into the blood. An elevated monocyte count doesn’t mean more soldiers; it means more officers. The canyon was demanding more eyes.

Persistently elevated for two years — the Marrow overproducing intelligence officers because the canyon kept demanding them. By April 2025 it touched normal for the first time. By November, settled in mid-range. The canyon no longer needs extra surveillance.

Platelets
The Marrow’s war-production gauge · ×1000/μL · Normal 214–375 (Yale) / 150–450 (Sinai)

Platelets are not immune cells. They plug wounds and clot blood. But they are made in the same Marrow as everything else, and when the Marrow is in a war-production state — driven by inflammatory signals like IL-6 — it ramps up all its production lines, not just immune cells. Persistently elevated platelets in IBD are called reactive thrombocytosis: a sign that the factories have been running hot for a long time. This was the slowest marker to normalize — consistent with reactive thrombocytosis being driven by chronic IL-6, one of the last environmental signals to subside as the canyon quiets.

Every Yale measurement was above the normal ceiling — two years of the Marrow running its factories hot. Under risankizumab, platelets dropped into the normal range: 388 → 349 → 337 → 353. The factories have finally cooled.

Eosinophils
Ruling out what this isn’t · % of WBC · Normal 0.9–6.8%

If the canyon’s war were driven by type 2 inflammation — the allergic, eosinophilic kind seen in atopic dermatitis or parasitic infections — eosinophils would be elevated. They are the specialist corps of the T2 immune response. Their absence tells you what kind of war this is.

The line that didn’t move. Low-normal throughout. This was never a T2 war. Th1/Th17: soldiers and conscripts, not the eosinophil corps. Consistent with Crohn’s.

Bowel wall thickness by segment
Watching the Builders · cm · June 2, 2025 ultrasound at Mt. Sinai · Abnormal ≥ 0.30 cm

The scope sees the surface of the Wall — granularity, ulcers, inflammation. The ultrasound sees the Wall itself: its thickness, its layers, the blood flow within it. Wall thickening is the signature of the Builders waking up — fibroblasts depositing collagen, the tissue remodeling that, once established, cannot be reversed. This is the surveillance of Agreement 3.

The scope saw UC-like surface inflammation everywhere. The ultrasound saw Crohn’s-like walls: right-sided predominance, the terminal ileum thickest of all. Critically: no segment crosses the 3mm abnormal threshold. The Builders are not yet awake. The scope sees the paint. The ultrasound sees the wall.
